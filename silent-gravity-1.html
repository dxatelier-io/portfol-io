Prologue

To the girl whose silence kept me

And even now, after everything, i find myself walking the same late streets, tracing the hollows where your laughter used to fit. Night folds in around me like someone tucking a blanket close, the city becomes a careful witness. I think of the way you moved across rooms like a secret, small and certain, and i wonder how a body can remember another body with such arithmetic, the elbow against the counter, the breath on a cold morning.

There is a memory in me of your hands catching light—soft, patient. There is a memory of a smile that ruined the quiet i had been protecting. I didn't know then that a smile could be a geography i would keep returning to, compassless and unafraid.
