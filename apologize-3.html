Glossary of Almost

Words we used, meanings we avoided

We: a grammar of convenience; plural when soft, singular when difficult.

Later: a suitcase under the bed, full of clothes that don’t fit the weather anymore.

Fine: a four letter word meaning “help me without making me say please.”

Home: the space where your hoodie hangs like a flag of a country we never founded.

Sorry: a key we pretended to lose, even as we traced its shape with our pockets.

I keep this glossary near the kettle. Every morning, steam underlines the margins and the paper curls, making a small smile at the corner-like it knows the punchline and forgives me for not laughing yet. I brew something sweet, pretend it’s peach jam in a cup, and whisper a practice sentence to the spoon, "At least, apologize to me, and i will meet you halfway across the bridge i built from the quiet."
