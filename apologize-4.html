Bridge, With Lanterns

Where the river slows down and names become gentle

Tonight, i lit small lanterns along the handrails of the bridge. They looked like commas, soft pauses in a sentence that was learning not to run. I stood at the center and practiced hello, then practiced a silence that wasn’t a wall. The water below kept repeating itself, patient and glittering, as if to say that some stories can be reread without breaking.

If you come here, you’ll find the path is already sanded smooth. I’ve left room for your footsteps, not as a surrender, but as an invitation to be human with me again. Bring no ceremony. Bring only the word that is both a bandage and a beginning. Bring it small, if you must. Bring it late. But bring it.

And if you cannot, i will keep the lanterns anyway. I will walk home slowly, counting the light as proof that i built something tender out of the ache. I will write the date in the margin, fold the page, and place it near the kettle. Tomorrow, i will learn a new synonym for staying.
