Portrait in Soft Colors

Of lips, eyes, and the ruin of ordinary days

You had soft lips that learned how to say small mercies and not the loud apologies i once imagined. Your eyes—pretty, distracted, like pages from a book i wanted to own— held light in a way that made my sentences stumble. When you smiled, lawlessness arrived without warning, the careful scaffolding of my plans would loosen, and i would allow myself to drift into the orbit of that smile, which was both invitation and exquisite sabotage.

I remember thinking, once, that love was a weather pattern. It came and it left, forecasts were weak promises. Then you left, and the world learned a new climate on my skin. Still, even in the quiet, i find the geometry of your face mapped in small gestures, how you folded the corner of a page, how you traced the rim of your mug with a thumb.
