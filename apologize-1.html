Prologue

A doorway of pink dusk and unfinished sentences

The evening arrived like a letter with no stamp, delivered, somehow, without permission. I stood at the threshold of our shared silence, counting the soft hums of the city as if they were apologies you never said out loud. The window kept a halo of warm light on the floor, and i traced it with my toes, pretending it was a map back to the moment we forgot how to be gentle.

If i had known that leaving is a language with too many synonyms, i would've memorized them all. Instead, i held on to fragments... the chipped mug, the lilac scrunchie on the chair, your name written like a constellation in the margins of my notebook. They made a galaxy small enough to fit in my pocket, but still too heavy to carry without bending.

This is not a grand trial. I don’t want your confession, your theatre of regrets, your late night epiphanies. I want the simple verb you kept behind your teeth. I want the softest truth... at least, apologize to me.
